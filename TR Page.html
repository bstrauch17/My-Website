Novel Rear Wing Geometry: Terps Racing

Background

In this project, I researched, designed and simulated novel rear wing geometries in order to improve downforce and decrease drag with the help of senior members of the team. Based on URANS images of the 2023 Terps Racing car (TR23), we identiied airflow over the main rear wing element collided with it at an angle. This due to the air being directed over and around the roll hoop and driver's head. We sought to utilize this airflow in order to increase the wings efficiency.

SLM printing process

Research and Design

Imitation is the finest form of flattery. I started looking at other FSAE cars to see if any other competitors used a curved rear wing like the one that we had in mind. A few cars did use it, in varying positions due to various needs. The decision to not alter the current height of the main foil was made as we already know the air direction at that point. The new element was designed such that it curved upwards to meet the air coming off the roll hoop, to "smooth" out the airflow.

Simulation & Testing

Utilizing STAR CCM+ for our CFD simulations, the new rear wing was implemented onto the car, and simulated at the average speed of 35 MPH.

TR23 V0.3.34.2 Model

Original analyses show a decrease in drag, but a correlating decrease in downforce. This was due to to the design not directing airflow to the secondary and tertiary elements, which is what the fundamental design of the wing depends on. The light blue areas indicate low pressure, which at the back of the car means drag. Further designs must recognize the airflow path, and incorporate things such as a flared trailing edge, altered angle of attack, or more.
